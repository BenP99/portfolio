David Pridmore Biography




David has recently joined Brunel Centre of Bedford Ltd. as a board member and trustee.


He spent his early working life working with the gauge and chronometer manufacturer Thomas Mercer of St. Albans. He then moved to British Aerospace in Hatfield and following fourteen years in engineering entered education in the role of lecturer. His career in education began as a lecturer in electrical engineering moving to the post of Head of Engineering at Bedford College. David was promoted to the role of Deputy Principal at Bedford College, a position he held for eighteen years before his recent retirement.


He has been involved in the delivery of vocational education, and in particular apprenticeships, with young people for over thirty years. David takes an active role in the development of apprenticeships and has been working on a range of new Government initiatives around vocational education.


He is also a trustee of the Learning Partnership in Bedford, which supports the voluntary sector.

Colin Steed Biography




Colin is the longest serving member of Council joining as a trustee in 1985 when the organisation was an Engineering Industry Training Board group training association. Colin held the chair from 1989 to 1994 and from 2009 to present.


Commitments as the national president of the Institute of British Foundrymen made it necessary to step down in the interim. During his first term he was responsible for the appointment of a new CEO who took the organisation through significant re-development and relocation.


Colin spent his entire career in engineering. As an apprentice pattern-maker with W.H.Allen & Co. Ltd. he represented the U.K at the International Apprentices Competition in 1965 winning a gold medal. After working in the foundry industry for several years he established an aluminium foundry in Bedford, specialising in the production of precision sand castings for use in the electronics, computer, and communications industries.

Brian Lawson Biography




Brian has been a council member for nearly thirty years, firstly in the role of Chief Executive to the training group from 1988 to 2007 and currently as CEO of the Brunel Centre. He developed the training group from an apprentice training school to a multi faceted award winning training provider and oversaw the transition to the present charity in 2014.


He spent his early working years with electronics manufacturer Texas Instruments Ltd. rising to the position of European Technical Services Manager for their semiconductor materials division. Brian started, then sold two businesses, one specialising in the reclaim of silicon epitaxial wafers and another designing and installing toxic gas detection systems.


Brian has worked abroad for a short time as a development engineer in Cape Town. He is named inventor on two patents.


Immediately prior to joining the training group Brian worked at the Cranfield Institute of Technology culminating as manager of its product engineering centre. He has had several articles published in the technical press.

John Ware Biography




John was born in Bedford, and after leaving Luton Technical College, joined Igranic Electrical Ltd. as a technical apprentice. On completion of his apprenticeship John qualified as an Electrical Draughtsman. He later became Section Leader and was subsequently promoted to Chief Draughtsman. The company changed its name, initially to BHI and then to Cutler Hammer, finally becoming part of the Eaton Corporation.


In 1987 Eaton closed its electrical controls division, ceasing the operation in Bedford. As a result of the closure John was made redundant. He, with six other directors formed their own company, Igranic Control Systems (ICS), manufacturing electrical switchgear. John took the role of Production Director in the new company. In 1990 ICS acquired a local company called PSJ Fabrications. PSJ were manufacturers of sheet metal enclosures, which were needed for ICS products, and also a very broad range of products in other markets. John took over as the Operations Manager of PSJ.


The takeover resulted in the formation of the Igranic Group PLC, John becoming a director and major shareholder. He also took on the role of Director of Health and Safety in the new group. The group grew to a turnover of £8 million per annum, employing a workforce of 70 to 80 employees. In 2012 the directors sold their Igranic Group PLC business to the Autotech Group and John retired. John brings a wealth of industrial experience to the charity.

Barry Watson Biography




Barry was educated at Bedford Modern School. On leaving he joined McPherson, Timmins and Edney, Chartered Accountants - latterly Thornton Baker. He then joined W.H.Allens where he served a commercial apprenticeship, covering all financial aspects in the heavy engineering business. He then moved on to Marston Valley Brick company again dealing with all financial business.


Following their takeover by London Brick Company he transferred to Stewartby and soon joined their internal audit department involving visiting all sites and ensuring integrity within the finances of the company. He also acted as relief manager in times of holidays and sickness.


When London Brick was taken over by Hanson Trust Barry was made redundant, but was soon taken back to the Landfill Division which he had previously helped to set up. Here he was area accountant, and then cost and management accountant responsible for budgetary control.


The company was then taken over by Shanks and McEwan and his responsibilities took him all over Great Britain. Barry was made redundant again in 1993, but taken back six weeks later to set up a new department, responsible for all plant maintenance and hire. He then moved into training and was responsible for introducing accounting systems to sites throughout the country.

Brunel Centre of Bedford Ltd.

The charitable aims of the charity are:

  • To provide premises and facilities for the provision of technical vocational and educational training course for the general public and particularly for the advancement and of the skills and knowledge of young people.

 

  • To award bursaries or grants to enable young people to access technical vocational training and education that may otherwise be unavailable to them.
